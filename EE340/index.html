Linear Polarization

Occurs when the phase difference δ = 0 or π. The electric field tip traces a straight line. The orientation depends on the ratio aᵧ/aₓ. No handedness is associated.

Circular Polarization

Requires aₓ = aᵧ and |δ| = 90° exactly. The magnitude remains constant while the angle rotates uniformly. Sign of δ determines handedness.

Elliptical Polarization

The general case — characterised by rotation angle γ and ellipticity angle χ. Left-hand if χ > 0, Right-hand if χ < 0.
