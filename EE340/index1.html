Polarization Type
Linear

Linear Polarization

Occurs when the phase difference δ = 0 or π. The electric field tip traces a straight line. The orientation depends strictly on the ratio of the amplitudes aᵧ/aₓ. There is no handedness associated with linear polarization.

Circular Polarization

Occurs when amplitudes are strictly equal (aₓ = aᵧ) and the phase difference is exactly |δ| = 90°. The magnitude remains constant while the angle rotates uniformly. Handedness (L/R) is determined by the sign of δ.

Elliptical Polarization

The most general case, characterized by the rotation angle (γ) and ellipticity angle (χ). The E-field vector traces an ellipse. Left-hand if χ > 0, Right-hand if χ < 0.
